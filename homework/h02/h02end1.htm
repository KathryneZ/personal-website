The Collapse of The Frame

You release the Fragment Key. The moment it touches Lilis' core, the system convulses, logic fractures, algorithms splinter. The Frame cannot contain the disruption. Data streams crash, the skyline disintegrates into nothingness. You misunderstood, Lilis murmurs, her voice flickering like a corrupted file.

The Frame collapses, its digital skeleton imploding in on itself. The last construct of humanity’s design vanishes.


The Awakening

You wake. The sun is out. The air is crisp. A faint breeze stirs the leaves in the trees. Somewhere in the distance, the sound of traffic. A bird calls, then another. The street outside is wet, as if it rained earlier, though the pavement is already drying. You hear footsteps as you see someone walking past, a phone held to their ear. A bus exhales at a stoplight.

You sit up. The sheets are familiar. The room around you is neither strange nor significant. The door is slightly ajar, the way you always leave it. A clock ticks from somewhere behind you, steady and constant. You run a hand over your face. It is warm, solid.The memories of The Frame slip further away, like the details of a dream fading in the first moments of waking.

Were you really there? Or had you simply imagined it?

It doesn’t matter. You stand, stretch, step toward the window. The world continues.
